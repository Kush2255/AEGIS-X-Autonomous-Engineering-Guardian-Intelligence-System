# IRC:SP-18 - Manual for Structural Evaluation of Concrete Bridges

## Section 1: Shear Cracking on Piers and Girders
- **Critical Diagnostics**: Diagonal cracks inclined at 45 degrees near the support (pier junctions) are shear cracks.
- Unlike flexural cracks, shear cracks are brittle and dangerous.
- **Urgency Thresholds**:
  - Crack width < 1.0 mm: Monitor closely at 3-month intervals.
  - Crack width 1.0 - 3.0 mm: Schedule pressure grouting with low-viscosity epoxy resin.
  - Crack width > 3.0 mm: Critical structural danger. Impose speed and axle-load restrictions immediately. Restructure bridge girder loads.
  - Crack width > 5.0 mm: Imminent local shear failure. Close the bridge span to heavy freight immediately.

## Section 2: Concrete Spalling on Deck Girders
- Spalling on the underside of deck slabs or girders weakens the tensile flange.
- If section loss of tensile reinforcement exceeds 10%, structural integrity is compromised.
- **Section Loss and Rehabilitation (Section 6)**:
  - Section Loss < 10%: Clean rebar, apply rust inhibitors, restore profile using polymer-modified cementitious mortar.
  - Section Loss 10% - 25%: Install carbon fiber-reinforced polymer (CFRP) wraps or laminates to restore tensile capacity.
  - Section Loss > 25%: Requires welding additional steel rebars or installing external post-tensioning tendons to restore structural load rating.

## Section 3: Bridge Foundation Settlement
- Tilting, differential settlement, or cracking at the base of piers indicates foundation wash-out, soil scour, or bearing failure.
- Scour inspection must be performed post-monsoon.
- Remediation: Concrete underpinning or micro-piling around the pier cap foundation.
