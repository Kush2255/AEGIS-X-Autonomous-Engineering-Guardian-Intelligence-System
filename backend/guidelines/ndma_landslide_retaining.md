# NDMA Guidelines: Landslide Risk Mitigation & Retaining Structures

## Section 1: Retaining Wall Structural Inspections
Retaining walls (masonry, reinforced concrete, or gabion) are critical for protecting highway roads, bridges, and mountain structures.
- **Defect Indicators**:
  - **Tilted Wall**: Indicates earth pressure exceeding design capacity or soil liquefaction behind the wall.
  - **Weep Hole Blockage**: Blocked weep holes trap water behind the wall, creating hydrostatic pressure. This is the #1 cause of retaining wall collapse.
  - **Horizontal Cracking**: Indicates bending stress. Cracks > 2.0 mm on concrete retaining walls require immediate pressure relief.
- **Remediation**:
  - Clear clogged weep holes. Drill new weep holes at 1.5m grid intervals.
  - Install ground anchors (soil nailing) to anchor the retaining wall back into stable rock.

## Section 2: Slope Stability and Soil Erosion
- Drainage structures, catch drains, and concrete-lined chutes must be free of debris.
- Soil slopes near highway bridge abutments require geotextile membrane covers or shotcrete application to prevent erosion under heavy monsoons.
- If cracks form in the soil slope crest, it indicates tension cracking and imminent landslide failure. Fill cracks with compacted clay immediately to prevent water ingress.
