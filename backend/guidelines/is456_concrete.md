# IS 456:2000 - Plain and Reinforced Concrete Code of Practice

## Section 1: Permissible Crack Widths
According to Clause 35.3.2, structural cracks in concrete structures affect durability and appearance. The limits for surface crack widths under serviceability limit state are:
- **Mild Exposure**: Surface crack width should not exceed **0.3 mm** in reinforced concrete members.
- **Moderate Exposure**: Surface crack width should not exceed **0.2 mm** (e.g., structures exposed to rain or damp basements).
- **Severe Exposure**: Surface crack width should not exceed **0.1 mm** (e.g., structures exposed to marine environments or aggressive chemicals).
- **Critical Structural Danger**: Any crack exceeding **1.5 mm** indicates shear distress or structural overloading. Immediate pressure epoxy injection is required.

## Section 2: Concrete Spalling and Reinforcement Exposure
Clause 26.4 states that concrete cover protects reinforcement from corrosion and fire.
- **Nominal Cover Requirements**:
  - Slab: 20 mm
  - Beam: 25 mm
  - Column: 40 mm
  - Foundation: 50 mm
- **Spalling Action Limits**:
  - When concrete spalls and reinforcement is exposed, the carbonation front has reached the steel.
  - Exposure of reinforcement bars leads to steel section loss. Active scaling rust reduces the tensile capacity.
  - **Clause 11.3 Repair Standard**: Expose the corroded steel completely, clean using wire brushes or sandblasting, apply a zinc-rich anti-corrosion primer, and patch with polymer-modified mortar or micro-concrete.

## Section 3: Concrete Leaching and Carbonation
- **Calcium Leaching**: White efflorescence or carbonate deposits on concrete surfaces (especially in tunnels or dams) indicate that water is leaching calcium hydroxide.
- Leaching increases concrete porosity, dropping compressive strength and paving the way for carbonation.
- Remediation: Drill injection ports and pump polyurethane chemical grout to stop water ingress.
